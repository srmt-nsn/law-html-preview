4.05.040 Manufacturing

(a) Requirement for a License. Any person(s) within the jurisdiction of this chapter, who wishes to produce tobacco products, cigarette papers, tubes, smokeless tobacco, pipe tobacco, or roll your-own tobacco, except for a person who produces tobacco products solely for that person's own consumption or use, requires a Saint Regis Mohawk Tribal Manufacturing license.

(b) Granting of Licenses. A license to manufacture tobacco products will only be granted to those persons whose principal business activity under such license will be the original manufacturer and will agree not to engage in business as a manufacturer without an approved license issued by the Saint Regis Mohawk Tribal Council while serving a suspension or closure. Such license shall only be approved for those individuals who qualify and comply with the rules and regulations issued hereunder. For all purposes intended, repackaging or re-labeling activities alone qualifies as a manufacturing activity and is subject to all applicable fees.

(c) License Approval or Denial. The Saint Regis Mohawk Tribe reserves the right to deny any application if such rejection is determined to be in the best interest of the Tribe. Reasons found before the granting of, or uncovered after the granting of a license, for automatic rejection include, but are not limited to, the submission of incomplete or false information in the application; the failure to disclose or meet the minimum criteria for the facility; a determination that the individual applicant or, in the case of a business entity, a partner, officer, director, stockholder is not likely to comply with this chapter, or pay the required fees to the Tribe; or otherwise willfully fail to operate the facility in an acceptable manner; or a determination that the approval will adversely impact the economic development of the Tribe or adversely impact the Tribe's relationship with other sovereign entities; or discrepancies are discovered during the investigation process of the applicant. If there is reason to believe that the applicant is not entitled to a license, the Compliance Director shall promptly notify the applicant of the contemplated disapproval and opportunity for a hearing with the Tribal Council.

(d) License Renewal.

The Saint Regis Mohawk Tribe reserves the right to deny any request for a license renewal and within 30-days before license expiration date the Compliance Department will notify Licensee by mail and include a renewal application. Upon return of renewal application the Compliance Department will review the licensees history, a recommendation is submitted with the application to the Tribal Council and a decision to renew is rendered by the Tribal Council.

(e) Application for a License.

(1) Every person or entity, before commencing business, as a manufacturer of tobacco products, shall submit an application for a license along with a security deposit of $50,000.00. In lieu of deposit, land or building may be used for collateral purposes.

(2) An individual applicant must be twenty-one (21) years of age or more and a member of the Saint Regis Mohawk Tribe. In the case of a business entity or partnership, the applicant must be formed and operated pursuant to the laws and regulations of the Saint Regis Mohawk Tribe. The applicant shall furnish with its application for license, a true copy of the articles of partnership or association, if any, or certificate of partnership or association.

(3) The application form at Appendix B must be completed and all requested information provided.

(f) Security Deposit

Each manufacturer's security deposit will be placed in escrow in an individual interest bearing account, which will never be co-mingled with any other tribal fund.

Contingent upon compliance with the provisions and regulations hereunder this deposit shall remain untouched in escrow for the life of the license, at which time it will be rolled over or returned plus interest earned minus any unresolved fees or fines due to the tribe.

Any deduction to an account will be based on a 30-day out basis. Licensee must bring account to balance before the issuance of a new license is permitted.

(g) Minimum Criteria for Manufacturing Facility. The premises to be used by a manufacturer of tobacco products as a factory may consist of more than one building, or portions of buildings, which need not be contiguous but must be located in the same territory. If any of the following conditions exist a diagram shall be furnished with application showing the information indicated:

  • Recommended facility size of at least 3000 sq. ft. and not adjacent to or connected to a building or structure used as a residence;
  • Where the factory is in more than one building, and each building is not identifiable by a separate address; identify each building by a letter, number or similar designation;
  • Facility must have adequate entrances, exits and fire extinguishers approved by the fire department;
  • Licensee shall operate only one manufacturing factory;
  • Licensee shall register with the Compliance Department the serial numbers of all cigarette producing machines;
  • Facility must have adequate sanitation for the size of staff;
  • Facility must have adequate security systems;
  • Facility must maintain adequate liability insurance for each building and staff therein;
  • Facility must comply with all environmental rules and regulations of the Saint Regis Mohawk Tribe.

(h) Manufacturers responsibility to Compliance Department

Should the Tribal Council deem it necessary for a Compliance Inspector to be stationed in the factory during all operating hours, it is the responsibility of the manufacturer to provide secure, reasonable accommodations for the inspectors.

(i) Distribution of Manufactured Cigarettes. A Tribally licensed manufacturer may sell its cigarettes to only authorized tribally licensed wholesalers in accordance with Section 5. The licensed wholesaler may sell such manufacturer's products to retailers within the Saint Regis Mohawk Reservation or in Nation-to-Nation sales within the United States to only those individuals/businesses on other Indian Reservations whose operation is in accordance with their respective Tribal laws and regulations. The Compliance Office shall provide each manufacturer with a list of currently authorized Saint Regis Mohawk Tribal licensed wholesalers.

(j) Annual Licensing Fee. Each Manufacturer shall pay an annual minimum fee of $2000.00 subject to an increase based on manufactures sales volume of previous year. No less or no more than a 30-day notice will be given to Licensee for renewal purposes. Should a lapse in license coverage occur, operation of manufacturing must cease and complete licensing process will be required. Any production without a license will be deemed in violation of this chapter and will be subject to Fines and Penalties.

(k) Reporting Requirements. The following shall be reported bi-weekly to the Compliance department:

(1) the number of cigarettes, by type, manufactured during the month. This information shall be provided on the form at Appendix C.

(2) cigarettes sold to tribal licensed wholesalers on the form at Appendix D.

(3) the Compliance Department may, at any time, conduct an inventory. In addition, the Compliance Department may request information on the source and amount of weekly or monthly sales of tobacco or other supplies to the manufacturer. The Compliance Department I s authorized to communicate with tobacco and other suppliers regarding the quantity of materials furnished to a licensed manufacturer or any other matters relevant to the licensees activities.

(4) All manufacturer records inclusive of; copies of authorizations, claims, inventories, notices, reports, returns and schedules will be retained in the Tribal Compliance Department in accordance with Tribal confidentiality policy.

(l) Cigarette Manufacturing Fee. Each manufacturer shall pay a Cigarette Manufacturing Fee on finished product which is assessed at $7.00 per case fee payable to the TGF office within the first week of each month. The submission of the monthly manufacturing report may be attached at time of payment or hand delivered to Compliance Office.

The payment of the fee shall be made within five (5) business days from the last day of the month. Failure to submit the report or to pay the fee on time shall result in a late fee of 5% of the Manufacturing Fee due. A failure to pay the fee for more than ten (10) business days will result in a 10% late charge and may serve as a basis for revocation or suspension of the license.

(m) Packages, Marks, Labels and Notices.

(1) Marks, Labels and Notices; Every package of tobacco products shall bear the marks, Medical Director Warning label, and other labels and notices, if any, that the Tribal Council prescribes.

(2) Indecent or Immoral Materials Prohibited; No indecent or immoral picture, print, or representation shall be attached to or stamped, marked, written or printed on any package of tobacco products.

(3) The manufacturer shall adhere to all safety regulations related to the use of fire-safe cigarette papers.

(n) Discontinuance of Operations. Every manufacturer of tobacco products who desires to discontinue operations under this part shall dispose of all tobacco products on hand and make a concluding inventory and concluding report. The manufacturer shall surrender his license with all concluding documents to the Compliance department as notice of such discontinuance.

(o) Suspension and revocation of License. Whereas the Compliance Department has reason to believe that a manufacturer of tobacco products has not in good faith complied with the Tribal Ordinance, rules and regulations and with intent to defraud, or has failed to disclose any material information required or made any material false statements in the application for the license or has failed to maintain his promises in such manner as to protect the revenue, the Compliance Director shall do an investigation and at the conclusion of such investigation shall present all pertinent findings and a recommendation to the Tribal General Counsel See Section 7.

(Res. No. 2004-40.)
